Gym Workouts

PPL (Push, Pull, Legs)

The PPL workout is a popular and effective way to structure your workouts. It splits your workout into three categories: Push (chest, shoulders, triceps), Pull (back, biceps), and Legs.

Example Day - Push

  • Bench Press: 4 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Tricep Dips: 3 sets of 10-15 reps

How to Perform Bench Press

Arnold Split

The Arnold Split is a workout routine designed by Arnold Schwarzenegger, focusing on a split routine that emphasizes both volume and intensity.

Example Day - Chest and Back

  • Flat Bench Press: 4 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Pull-Ups: 4 sets of 10 reps

How to Perform Flat Bench Press

Pro Split

The Pro Split workout divides training into separate days focusing on different muscle groups, aiming for higher frequency and volume.

Example Day - Arms

  • Bicep Curls: 4 sets of 10-15 reps
  • Tricep Extensions: 4 sets of 10-15 reps
  • Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 12 reps

How to Perform Bicep Curls

Every Day Muscle

Every Day Muscle is a training philosophy that involves working out daily with a focus on improving muscular endurance and hypertrophy.

Example Day - Full Body

  • Squats: 4 sets of 12 reps
  • Push-Ups: 4 sets of 15 reps
  • Pull-Ups: 3 sets of 10 reps
